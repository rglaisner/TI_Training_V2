# TI Training V2 - First-Time User Guide

## 1) What TI Training V2 Is

TI Training V2 is a practical simulation environment for Talent Intelligence (TI), Workforce Planning, and People Analytics professionals.

Instead of reading theory slides, you enter realistic mission situations and make decisions under pressure, just like in real business contexts. Your choices and written responses are evaluated, and your progress is tracked over time.

Think of it as a **mission control simulator for TI decisions**:

- You face realistic stakeholder pressure.
- You work with incomplete or conflicting signals.
- You are evaluated on decision quality, communication, and integrity.

This platform is designed for:

- experienced TI practitioners who want sharper strategic impact
- newer associates who want a clear and credible way to build judgment fast

---

## 2) How V2 Evolved from V1 (and Why It Matters to You)

V1 proved the concept: scenario-based TI training can improve judgment.  
V2 is the operational upgrade that makes the experience more robust, consistent, and enterprise-ready.

### What changed

1. **From prototype flow to full mission system**
   - V1 was more experimental.
   - V2 organizes learning into mission sessions with cleaner start, progress, and end states.

2. **From basic interaction to stronger evaluation logic**
   - V1 focused mainly on interactive practice.
   - V2 adds stronger scoring consistency for both choices and free-text responses.

3. **From limited tracking to meaningful progression**
   - V1 had less persistent progression structure.
   - V2 tracks competencies, certifications, evidence highlights, and trend signals in a clearer way.

4. **From one-size-fits-all pressure to adaptive challenge**
   - V2 adjusts challenge level based on demonstrated capability, so scenarios stay relevant over time.

### What this means for first-time users

- You get a more reliable and fair evaluation experience.
- You can see what improved and what needs work.
- You can replay and still be challenged, instead of repeating identical difficulty forever.

## 3) Your First Session: Step-by-Step

Use these steps exactly for your first 30 minutes.

### Step 1 - Enter the platform

1. Open the TI Training V2 landing page.
2. Sign in (or use guest mode if available in your environment).
3. Wait for the dashboard to load.

What you should see:

- a mission-control style home view
- available scenarios
- featured or recommended scenario cards

### Step 2 - Pick your first scenario

1. Open the scenario list.
2. Choose one scenario that matches your confidence level (start with a foundational or analyst-level scenario if unsure).
3. Click **Start Mission**.

What happens next:

- a mission session is created
- the first node/context appears in the Mission view

### Step 3 - Understand the mission screen quickly

On the Mission view, focus on four areas:

1. **Narrative context**
   - What is happening, who is involved, what is at risk.

2. **Action input**
   - You may get either a branching choice or an open text prompt.

3. **Social pressure layer**
   - Mentor, crowd, or roaming interactions may appear.
   - These simulate stakeholder dynamics and pressure.

4. **Session status**
   - Follow mission progress until terminal/summary state appears.

### Step 4 - Submit decisions the right way

1. Read the prompt once for urgency and stakeholder context.
2. Read it a second time for decision criteria.
3. Respond in concise business language.
4. Click submit once and wait for the updated node.

Good first-response pattern:

1. State your conclusion first.
2. Give evidence in 1-3 concrete points.
3. Explain the risk or trade-off.
4. Propose a practical next step.

### Step 5 - Finish the mission and review outcomes

1. Continue through nodes until the mission ends.
2. Open your summary/dossier output.
3. Review:
   - score feedback
   - demonstrated/not demonstrated outcome
   - competency impact

### Step 6 - Check your progress tracker

1. Open Tracker/Progress.
2. Review your:
   - certification level progress
   - competency trends
   - XP and engagement track
   - recent evidence highlights

3. Select one area to improve before your next mission.

## 5) How Evaluation Works (Non-Technical)

You are evaluated in two ways:

1. **Branching decisions**
   - The platform checks the consequence logic of your choice path.

2. **Open-text responses**
   - The platform evaluates response quality against TI competencies and scenario goals.

Each open response returns three core outcomes:

- **Score**: quality level of the response
- **Demonstrated**: whether minimum expected capability was shown
- **Feedback**: coaching on what improved or missed

### What the system typically rewards

- clear recommendation early (not buried)
- evidence-backed reasoning
- strong data integrity
- stakeholder-aware communication
- practical action orientation

### What usually causes low performance

- vague wording with no evidence
- long explanations with no conclusion
- avoiding the hard decision
- sacrificing integrity under pressure
- providing opinion without business relevance

## 6) What Matters Most in This Evaluation Environment

Use this checklist before every submission:

1. **Clarity first** - Lead with the bottom line.
2. **Evidence second** - Anchor points in available data/signals.
3. **Integrity always** - Never bend numbers to satisfy pressure.
4. **Decision ownership** - Make a call and explain trade-offs.
5. **Stakeholder realism** - Adapt tone to audience without losing rigor.
6. **Actionability** - End with what should happen next.

### Common mistakes and fixes

- **Mistake:** Writing a full analysis before the recommendation.  
  **Fix:** Put the recommendation in sentence one.

- **Mistake:** Overloading with detail to look smart.  
  **Fix:** Keep three high-impact points max.

- **Mistake:** Being technically right but politically tone-deaf.  
  **Fix:** Keep the message firm, professional, and usable for decision-makers.

## 7) Your First 30-Minute Success Path

If you are brand new, do this in order:

1. Sign in and open Dashboard.
2. Start one foundational scenario.
3. Complete one full mission without rushing.
4. Review feedback and identify one repeated weakness.
5. Run a second mission and focus only on that weakness.
6. Check Tracker and confirm whether trend direction improved.

If you do only this, you already build a strong first-week baseline.

## 8) Role-Based Hints

### If you are an experienced TI practitioner

- Treat each mission like a board-prep simulation, not a quiz.
- Show strategic trade-offs explicitly.
- Resist overconfidence: concise evidence beats authority tone.

### If you are an out-of-college associate

- Do not try to sound senior; try to sound clear and structured.
- Use a simple response frame: conclusion -> 2 facts -> action.
- Consistency beats complexity in early missions.

## 9) FAQ for First-Time Users

### 1) Is this a pass/fail training?

No. It is a progression system. You receive structured outcomes and can improve over multiple missions.

### 2) Are my open-text answers judged randomly?

No. The platform uses a fixed evaluation contract with defined outcomes (score, demonstrated, feedback) to keep responses consistent.

### 3) What if I make a wrong decision?

That is expected. Misses are part of the training signal. Review feedback, then replay with one focused improvement target.

### 4) What does "demonstrated" really mean?

It means your response met the minimum capability standard for that mission context and target competencies.

### 5) Do I need advanced technical skills to do well?

No. Strong TI judgment, clear communication, and evidence-backed reasoning matter more than technical depth.

### 6) Where do I see my long-term progress?

In Tracker/Progress, where you can inspect level movement, competency trends, and recent evidence highlights.

### 7) Can I replay scenarios?

Yes. Replays are useful because challenge can adapt to your profile, giving you fresh pressure patterns over time.

### 8) How do I improve fastest?

After each mission, choose one weakness, then run the next mission with that one improvement goal only.

---

## Final Note

TI Training V2 is most effective when used as a regular practice system, not a one-time certification event.

If you complete short, focused sessions and review your tracker trends weekly, you will build stronger strategic judgment and better stakeholder impact faster than with passive learning formats.
