# TIC Trainer V2: Certification & Reward Architecture

The TIC Trainer V2 platform abandons traditional multiple-choice grading in favor of a multidimensional, AI-evaluated progression system. To balance professional credibility with high user engagement, the platform utilizes a **Dual-Track Value Economy* **. This decouples "Serious" metrics (validated skills, certifications) from "Fun" metrics (XP, gamification).

__The goal is to provide enterprise leaders with an evidence-based map of organizational readiness, while giving practitioners a persistent, highly credible record of excellence.__

---

## Core Certification (Levels 0–6)
The core certification track measures a user's direct proficiency in Talent Intelligence (TI), Workforce Planning, and People Analytics. Progression is strictly governed by the `Backend Evaluator` analyzing free-text inputs and decision logic against the central Competency Catalog.

### The Hierarchy of Mastery
Users progress through seven distinct bands based on their aggregated competency scores:
* **Level 0:** Foundation / Sourcer
* **Level 1-2:** Analyst
* **Level 3-4:** Senior Analyst / Team Lead
* **Level 5:** TI Lead
* **Level 6:** Director / Strategic Architect

### Medal System (Scenario Performance)
Completion of a scenario is not a binary pass/fail. Performance is graded based on the ratio of "Best Practice/Good" decisions versus "Partial/Risk/Miss" actions, resulting in a tiered medal:
* **Distinction:** Complete + 90% Quality score. Unlocks high-tier Profile Labels.
* **Merit:** Complete + 70% Quality score.
* **Pass:** Complete (Minimum viable navigation of the scenario).

---

## 2. The Fun Track: Engagement & The Experience Economy
To prevent gamification fatigue while maintaining momentum, standard interactions generate Experience Points (XP). 

* **XP Accumulation:** Users earn base XP for completing nodes, with "Timed Bonuses" awarded for making high-quality decisions under simulated pressure.
* **Cosmetics & Perks:** XP can be spent on virtual perks or profile cosmetics. In V2, these cosmetics can passively alter the simulation—for example, equipping a specific "title" or "hat" might prompt an NPC to casually reference it during dialogue, reinforcing the platform's dynamic responsiveness.

---

## "Specialty" System (Passive Skill Recognition)
Talent Intelligence professionals often possess tangential superpowers—skills that fall outside the core TI mandate but drastically amplify their output. 

Instead of forcing users to take arbitrary tests to prove these skills, the platform uses a **Passive Listening Model**. Tangential skills are treated as "Easter Eggs" or "Observer Badges." The LLM is instructed to listen for unprompted, expert-level application of these domains in the user's free-text reasoning.

### Recognized Specialties (non exhaustive and not final):
* **Coding and Development:** (e.g., Proposing a Python script or custom API bridge to solve a data gap).
* **Data Engineering:** (e.g., Designing complex data lake architecture or ETL pipelines to merge ATS and HRIS data).
* **Design and UI Mastery:** (e.g., Wireframing a bespoke executive dashboard rather than relying on standard reporting).
* **System/Critical Thinker:** (e.g., Applying Monte Carlo simulations or probabilistic models to standard headcount requests).
* **Operational Excellence:** (e.g., Designing scalable SLA frameworks for intelligence intake).
* **AI Prompting Mastery:** (e.g., Demonstrating advanced prompt engineering to triage raw qualitative intelligence).
* **Business Fluency:** (e.g., Translating HR metrics strictly into revenue, EBITDA, or market-share impact).

**The UX Impact:**
Specialties badges are granted for exceptionally rare display of distinct mastery of tangentiak skills: they cannot be "grinded". When a user successfully applies a specialty in a scenario, they receive a post-mission notification (e.g., *"System detected advanced structural logic. 'Data Engineering' Specialty Awarded."*). This creates moments of unexpected delight and ensures the user's ultimate certification profile is as unique as their actual human skillset.
